sheherazade

Visit Sheherazade at any time day or night from the sidewalks of West Magnolia Avenue in the garage behind 1401 South 3rd Street, Louisville KY, 40208.

Sheherazade is a window gallery within an Old Louisville garage that exhibits large-scale works of art and site-specific installations and is only viewable from the outside. Guests may only enter the space during a one-time-only Open House for each site-specific installation. Installations will rotate every six weeks.

Sheherazade is an experiment by Julie Leidner with help from many people, with the intent:

  • To provide an alternative, fluid space for individual artists to share their non-traditional and site-specific projects with the public
  • To interrupt the boundaries of public and private space in a way that makes outsiders feel like insiders, insiders feel like outsiders, and that causes personal introspection
  • To coexist with, and to engage in cultural exchange with, the neighborhood of Old Louisville
  • To prioritize risk, abjection, and subversion over safety, complacency, and politeness in art
  • To prioritize art that raises questions and challenges the status quo
  • To amplify voices of artists who may not have already had significant exposure in the city/region
  • To exhibit emerging artists and artists who may be marginalized due to their race, ethnicity, sexual orientation, gender expression, or economic status.
  • To be and prioritize art that is primarily non-commercial, and not profit-driven
